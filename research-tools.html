What are AI Research Tools?

AI research tools are applications that use artificial intelligence to help find, analyze, and synthesize information from various sources. They include AI search engines like Perplexity that provide direct answers with citations, academic research assistants like Elicit that analyze scientific papers, and document analysis tools like NotebookLM that let you chat with your uploaded documents. These tools excel at synthesizing information from multiple sources, extracting key findings, and providing traceable citations.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What are AI research tools?
AI research tools are software that use artificial intelligence to help find, analyze, and synthesize information. They include AI-powered search engines like Perplexity, academic research assistants like Elicit, and document analysis tools like NotebookLM.
Is Perplexity better than Google for research?
For research requiring synthesis and citations, yes. Perplexity provides direct answers with sources rather than links to explore. For quick factual lookups and local searches, Google remains superior. Many researchers use both.
What's the best AI tool for academic research?
For scientific literature, Elicit and Consensus are the top choices. Elicit excels at finding papers and extracting findings; Consensus shows scientific agreement. Semantic Scholar is the best free option with 200M+ papers.
Can AI research tools replace traditional literature reviews?
AI tools significantly accelerate literature reviews but don't fully replace human judgment. They excel at finding relevant papers and extracting key findings, but researchers should still verify accuracy, assess methodology quality, and synthesize findings critically.
